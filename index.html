about

a little bit of code, a little bit of story.

I started in Computer Science and switched into Digital Narrative & Interactive Design because I realized I cared way more about whether something actually felt good to use than whether it technically worked. Lucky for me, I didn't have to pick one. These days I'm just as happy deep in backend logic as I am fussing over a user flow or a single line of code. Most of the projects I gravitate toward start with someone I know, usually a Pitt student, complaining about something very specific.

When I'm not doing that, I'm probably winning a board game against my friends, halfway through a puzzle, or planning a trip somewhere new. I'm so invested in learning new lanuages and I love the process! I speak English, Telugu, and Spanish and have started learning Portuguese. Give me a good playlist, a beauty tutorial, or an excuse to sing along to something and I'm set.
